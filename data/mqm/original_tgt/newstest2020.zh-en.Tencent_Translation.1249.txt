For nearly two months, the restoration of the large-scale mural "Tian Hou Temple Map" in Tianjin Tianhou Palace, a key national cultural protection unit, was announced to end on the 26th and will be reopened to the public during this year's National Day.
"Tian Hou Temple Map" is a large-scale traditional mural that comprehensively reflects Mazu's life.
With the approval of the relevant state departments, it was drawn by Cai Changkui, former director of Tianjin Folklore Museum (Tianhou Palace), from 1991 to 1994.
It is understood that this mural is painted on the south and north walls of the main hall of the Tianhou Palace. It is 32 meters long and 2.6 meters high. It is divided into two parts. It uses more than 30 stories such as the birth of the queen, the crossing of the sea and the ascension of the feather. 358 characters reproduce the life stories of Mazu.
Cai Changkui introduced that in recent years, Tianjin has been rainy in summer and dry in winter. This "wet and dry" has led to the rupture of the murals, which accounts for 60% of the total mural area.
In order to protect and rescue the murals of Mazu's deeds, the Management Committee of Ancient Culture Street in Nankai District, Tianjin and the Tianhou Palace launched the restoration project of the Tianhou Palace Hall and the murals in the temple.
The restoration project was led by Cai Changkui and invited four famous Chinese painters to participate together.
The restoration work was based on the principle of "building old as old". At the same time, the original murals were partially adjusted, and the original murals were not perfect.
In the restoration, we have also made innovations and attempts in color, materials, pen and ink.
After the restoration, the style, spirituality, spirit and artistic style of the original murals were maintained.
The Tianhou Palace was built in 1326. Originally known as the Tianfei Palace, commonly known as the Lady's Palace, has been rebuilt many times. It is the oldest complex in Tianjin. It is also one of the earliest Mazu temples in China.

Be careful to make tea with plastic tea bags!
According to the "Central News Agency" a recent study in Canada found that a bag of plastic tea bags may release billions or even tens of billions of plastic particles, significantly higher than the estimated amount of microplastics consumed by a person throughout the year, but so far there is no evidence that the ingestion of plastic particles will pose a threat to human beings.
It is reported that researchers at McGill University in Canada found that by immersing plastic tea bags in a cup of almost boiling hot water (95 degrees Celsius), tea bags will release about 11.6 billion plastic particles and 3.1 billion smaller nano-plastic particles into the cup.
This amount is significantly higher than the estimated amount of microplastics consumed by a person throughout the year.
According to research published earlier in 2019, the average person eats at least 50,000 plastic particles a year and inhales the same amount of microplastics.
It is reported that the study was published in the Journal of Environmental Science and Technology. The researchers tested four different types of plastic commercial tea bags in Montreal stores and cafes, opened the tea bags, washed the tea bags and immersed them in almost boiling water for five minutes, and analyzed them with an electron microscope and spectroscope.
According to the study, they found that a single tea bag released more than 11.6 billion plastic particles, "several orders of magnitude higher than the previous plastic load of other foods".
According to the report, most tea bags are made of natural fibers, but many tea bag brands that use natural fibers still use plastic to seal.
Some brand tea bags are no longer made of paper and have turned to plastic, which are the subjects tested by the researchers.
Plastic particles are tiny fragments of plastics. Researchers define these microplastics to be between 100 nm and 5 mm in size, mainly produced by the decomposition of plastic waste.
Researchers have found traces of plastic particles in air, soil, rivers and deepest trenches around the world, as well as in tap water, bottled water, seafood and beer.
In October 2018, researchers first found plastic particles in human excrement samples from several countries in different regions of the world.
To test the potential toxicity of plastic particles released from tea bags, the Canadian research team put daphnia into contaminated water and found that microplastics can affect the behavior of these daphnia and cause them to develop deformities.
According to the World Health Organization (WHO), there is no evidence so far that the ingestion of plastic particles poses a threat to human beings.
However, WHO pointed out that more research is needed to fully understand how these plastics spread into the environment and what impact they have on human beings.
The researchers speculate that the reason why these tea bags release a large number of plastic particles may be that these plastic tea bags come into contact with boiling water. Even when "edible grade" plastic is heated to more than 40 degrees Celsius, it may break down or seep out toxic substances.

During the year, the repurchase scale of listed companies exceeded 100 billion yuan to a record high - Xinhua
In order to stabilize the company's share price and maintain the company's image, listed companies have begun to buy back shares frequently in the past two years. Through targeted buybacks and collective bidding transactions, they hope to convey the information that the company's value is undervalued to the market, thus stabilizing the share price and enhancing investor confidence.
According to Oriental Wealth Choice data, the funds used by listed companies to buy back shares amounted to 7.9 billion yuan in 2017, soaring to 51 billion yuan in 2018. Since 2019, the total amount of repurchase implementation of A-share listed companies has exceeded 100 billion yuan, reaching a new high in the amount of A-share repurchase funds, and far exceeding previous years.
From the point of view of the purpose of repurchase, the company's repurchase of shares seems to be the same as the increase of major shareholders, but there are great differences in the main body of implementation, the source of funding, the purpose of repurchase, the rights and interests of the shares themselves, etc.
Xu Yang, chairman of Shanghai Maikorong Information Consulting Co., Ltd. said in an interview with a reporter from the Securities Daily that the "Opinions on Supporting Listed Companies to Repurchase Shares" issued in November 2018, repaired the problems existing in the current system, such as the narrow scope of share repurchase, complex decision-making procedures and inadequate inventory system. As a result of the previous policy to encourage listed companies to repurchase shares, the phenomenon of share repurchase increased significantly that year.
In January this year, the Shanghai Stock Exchange and the Shenzhen Stock Exchange officially issued the "Rules for the Implementation of the Repurchase of Shares by Listed Companies". The introduction of these policies has broadened the sources of repurchase funding for listed companies, properly simplified implementation procedures, guided and improved governance arrangements, and encouraged all types of listed companies to implement equity incentives or employee shareholding plans.
After the opinion was issued, the operation of share repurchase by listed companies has more applicable circumstances, the repurchase process is easier, and the enthusiasm for share repurchase is greater.
The repurchase of shares in a relatively low position will also help listed companies to improve the efficiency of the use of funds, thus increasing the level of earnings per share, raising the rate of return on net assets and reducing the pressure on the company's profit indicators.
Compared with the increase of major shareholders, the repurchase of shares by listed companies is of greater significance. It also reflects the willingness of listed companies to actively manage market value and abundant cash flow.
Xu Yang believes that the undervalued share price is the main factor in the repurchase of listed companies. The company's share price can no longer reflect the company's future development prospects. Therefore, such companies deserve the attention of investors, but they cannot invest blindly. They need to pay attention to the financial results and specific operating conditions of such listed companies.
Listed companies with large-scale repurchase behavior at least show the company's development confidence and financial strength.
The gradual warming of the repurchase enthusiasm of A-share listed companies has also normalized the repurchase behavior. The data show that nearly a thousand listed companies have implemented share repurchase this year, and the leading companies in various industries have become the main force of the repurchase market.
China Ping An recently disclosed the progress of repurchase, saying that the company has repurchased a total of 57.59 million A-share shares through centralized bidding trading, accounting for 0.315% of the company's total share capital. The study is based on the conclusions of a health survey of 44,000 adults conducted by the US government from 1999 to 2016.
"Despite considerable improvements, significant dietary challenges remain," the authors write.
It is reported that these challenges include reducing the intake of snacks, hot dogs, fat cattle, butter and other foods containing saturated fat.

The biggest improvement in the American national diet is the reduction in sugar intake from 16% to about 14%, but the intake is still too high.

The 34.5-kilometer-long Darui Railway Gaoligong Mountain tunnel is located in Yunnan. The tunnel runs through the Gaoligong Mountain Range from the west bank of the Nu River. It is the longest double-line single-hole tunnel in Asia.
The tunnel runs through 19 fault zones, with a maximum depth of 1,155 meters, because it covers almost all the bad geology and major risks that may be encountered in tunnel construction. The Gaoligongshan tunnel is known as the "geological museum" of tunnel construction.
The shaft is the most important auxiliary tunnel of Gaoligong Mountain tunnel. It undertakes the important task of increasing the working surface to realize "long tunnel short hit" and later railway operation ventilation.
The No. 1 shaft was successfully excavated to the bottom of the well, marking the official entry of the Gaoligongshan tunnel into the horizontal tunnel construction. After the bottom yard is excavated and the equipment is replaced, four working surfaces can be added underground to speed up the excavation speed of the main tunnel.
"The shaft is all in the granite strata. Because there are many cracks in the granite strata, the water cannot be stopped. It flows from top to bottom. Basically, the water pressure is as large as the depth of the well."
Shi Jingzhao, chief engineer of the Darui Railway Project Department of the Railway Tunnel Bureau, told the reporter that during the construction of the shaft, there were many dangerous situations such as mud inrush and collapse. The construction party invited experts to take the pulse on the spot and discussed the treatment plan more than 20 times.
"When the water inrush in the shaft is maximum, it reaches more than 300 cubic meters per hour, the bottom of the well is more than 700 meters deep, and the water pressure is equivalent to the seabed 700 meters deep."
Si Jingzhao said, "If we do not pump and drain in time, the well will be flooded by 10 meters in an hour. We spend almost 85% of our time on water control and 15% of our time on digging."
In order to overcome geological problems such as longitudinal joint development of surrounding rock of No. 1 shaft, mud inrush and rich-water artered rock, the tunnel construction adopts systematic water control measures combining "exploration, injection and excavation" to reduce the construction risk.
After many improvements, the construction of shaft No. 1 has formed mechanized assembly lines such as excavation system, ballast system, suspension system and winch lifting system, realized the full process of mechanization operations such as eye drilling, blasting, ballast, template installation and concrete pouring, and accelerated the construction progress.
Finally, after more than 1200 days and nights of construction, the shaft was successfully excavated at a depth of 762.59 meters.

Before that, Chinese high school graduates who want to apply for German universities need to study for one semester (Project 211 and 985 universities) or three semesters (general undergraduate) in China, and pass the APS examination or TestAS examination.
The promulgation of the new rules may save at least half a year for Chinese students who are willing to study in Germany.
It is reported that the new rules will come into effect in the second half of 2019.
Applicants are required to meet: complete 12 years of formal compulsory education and obtain a high school graduation certificate; participate in the HKCEE and college entrance examination, and the college entrance examination score reaches 70% or more of the total score (if the total score is 750, it must reach more than 525 points), and it includes two language scores and one mathematics or natural science score.
In addition, the requirements of specific specialties for college entrance examination results are different.
For example, when applying for a major in medicine, stomatology, veterinary medicine, pharmacy, etc., the college entrance examination score must reach 80% of the full score, and the college entrance examination subjects must include at least two subjects in mathematics, physics, chemistry, biology, or participate in integrated science; when applying for a major in law, the college entrance examination score must reach 80% of the full score, and the college entrance examination subjects must include at least two subjects in foreign languages, history, geography, or participate in comprehensive literature.

The reporter learned from the Yunnan Academy of Agricultural Sciences on the 27th that the spodoptera frugiperda in Yunnan has been effectively prevented and controlled, which has not caused a large-scale outbreak of disaster, and the prevention and control work has achieved stage results.
In January this year, Jiangcheng County, Pu'er City, Yunnan Province, first discovered the spodoptera frugiperda migrating from abroad. Subsequently, it was confirmed in 129 counties in Yunnan. The spodoptera frugiperda also gradually spread from Yunnan to the Mainland of China, posing a direct threat to China's grain production.
The spodoptera frugiperda is an important long-distance migratory agricultural pest native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It is a major migratory pest warned by the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations. It has the characteristics of "international, migratory, violent and destructive".
The pest harms more than 80 crops such as corn, sugar cane and sorghum. It takes a large amount of food and can cause up to 70% of the yield loss.
The prevention and control is very difficult, and there are no registered pesticides to control the insect in China.
Since the spodoptera frugiperda migrated and invaded Yunnan, the Yunnan Academy of Agricultural Sciences selected Chen Aidong researcher to lead the Yunnan Academy of Agricultural Sciences agricultural cross-border pests in collaboration with the green prevention and control provincial innovation team to go to the front line, investigate on the ground, collect samples, and carry out technical guidance and experimental demonstration.
Chen Aidong's team has initially mastered the population characteristics of spodoptera frugiperda in Yunnan, natural enemy insect resources, overseas insect source characteristics, population migration trajectory, drug toxicity and field prevention and control effect.
They have actively carried out the research and application of green prevention and control technology. Through the implementation of new green prevention and control technologies such as population monitoring, adult trapping, scientific drug use and agronomic measures, the prevention and control effect in the demonstration zone has reached more than 90%. At the same time, they have reduced the use of chemical pesticides, protected natural enemy resources and ecological environment, and provided a technical guarantee for Yunnan to effectively control the outbreak and sustainable management of the green night moth.
At present, Yunnan spodoptera frugiperda has been effectively prevented and controlled, which has not caused a large-scale outbreak of disaster, and the prevention and control work has achieved stage results.
In the next step, the Yunnan Academy of Agricultural Sciences will focus on the ecological mechanism of population migration and invasion, and break through the construction of biological control and green prevention and control technology system.
At the same time, we will actively promote cross-border international cooperation and strive to establish joint key laboratories and joint monitoring network points and demonstration zones abroad in order to enhance the all-round monitoring and early warning capabilities of Yunnan spodoptera frugiperda.

New rules to solicit opinions: social capital needs to be qualified to obtain rural land management rights
Social capital needs to be qualified to obtain rural land management rights
"Measures for the Administration of the Transfer of Rural Land Management Rights" Public Consultation to supervise the transfer of social capital such as enterprises to obtain land management rights
Yesterday, the Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs website published a notice on the "Management Measures for the Transfer of Rural Land Management Rights (Revised Draft for Consultation)" (hereinafter referred to as the "Draft for Consultation"), which increased the supervision and risk prevention of the transfer of industrial and commercial capital to obtain land management rights.
The Draft Opinion will now be open to the public for comment.
According to the Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs, in order to implement the newly revised Law on Land Contract in Rural Areas, further consolidate and improve the basic rural management system, deepen the reform of the rural land system and implement the "separation of three rights" system, the Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs revised the "Measures for the Administration of the Transfer of Rural Land Contract Management Rights" which formed the "Draft Opinions".
The Beijing Youth Daily reporter learned that there were many important changes to the Draft Opinion published for the first time.
The newly revised Law on Land Contract in Rural Areas clarifies the scope of land transfer and implements the concept of "separation of three rights". Special sections set up "land contract management rights" and "land management rights" respectively, making a clear distinction between the transfer, exchange of land contract management rights and the transfer of land management rights (rental, subcontracting, equity or other means).
In this "Opinions Draft" in order to implement the concept of "separation of three rights" effectively respond to the main thrust of the amendment of the Law on Land Contract in Rural Areas, and take into account the great differences between the transfer of land management rights and the exchange and transfer of land contract management rights in terms of parties, procedures and supervision, this revised draft defines the scope of circulation, focusing on the transfer of land management rights, including rental, equity, etc., and amends the name of the regulations from the "Measures for the Administration of the Transfer of Rural Land Contract Management Rights" to the "Measures for the Administration of the Transfer of Rural Land Management Rights".
The transfer and exchange of land contract management rights will be regulated by separate management measures.
The Draft Opinion points out that under the background of "separation of three rights" farmers only take a stake in the right to land management for a certain period of time.
Theoretically, even if there is bankruptcy liquidation, farmers only have the risk of losing their management rights for a certain period of time. Therefore, it is not necessary in jurisprudence to stipulate that the contracted land should be returned to the original contractor, and there is a conflict with the Bankruptcy Law.
Aiming at the problem of preventing the risk of farmers losing land, methods such as preferred shares, first rental and then repurchase have emerged in practice.
Considering that the preferred stock has a mature theoretical discussion in the company law, it is suggested that it be used as an advocacy provision to prevent the risk of losing land.
